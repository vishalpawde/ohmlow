AMPERES (A)
⚡
Voltage (V)
Electrical potential difference that drives current through a conductor. Higher voltage = stronger push.
〰️
Current (I)
Flow of electric charge (electrons) per second. Measured in Amperes. It's the effect of voltage overcoming resistance.
🔩
Resistance (R)
Opposition to current flow in a circuit. Materials like copper have low resistance; insulators have very high resistance.
